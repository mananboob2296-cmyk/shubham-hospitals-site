<p><em>Medically reviewed by <a href="/dr-murlidhar/">Dr. Murlidhar Boob</a> — MS (Surgery), FAIS, DLS, FMAS, Laparoscopic Surgeon &amp; Surgical Gastroenterologist.</em></p>

<p>In three decades of surgical practice, I've noticed something odd. Patients will ask me detailed questions about anaesthesia, stitches, and recovery — and then hesitate at the one question sitting heaviest on their mind: <em>"Doctor, what will all this cost?"</em> So let me answer it the way I do across the desk: openly, with real ranges, and with an honest explanation of why the same operation can carry two very different price tags.</p>

<h2>Why there is never one fixed price</h2>

<p>Laparoscopic (keyhole) surgery uses specialised equipment — a camera, insufflation, fine instruments, and often consumables like staplers, clips, or mesh that are used once and discarded. The final bill is a package of the surgeon's fee, anaesthesia, operation theatre charges, those consumables, your room category, and the length of stay. Change any one of these and the number moves. That's not hospitals being cagey; it's genuinely how the arithmetic works.</p>

<h2>Surgery-wise ranges you can actually use</h2>

<p>These are broad, indicative package ranges for India as of 2026. Smaller cities generally sit toward the lower end, large corporate hospitals in metros toward the upper end. Your own quote may fall outside them for reasons we'll come to — treat these as a sanity check, not a rate card.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic laparoscopy</strong> (a look inside to find the cause of pain or infertility): roughly ₹25,000–₹60,000</li>
<li><strong>Laparoscopic appendicectomy</strong> (appendix removal): roughly ₹35,000–₹90,000</li>
<li><strong>Laparoscopic cholecystectomy</strong> (gallbladder removal): roughly ₹40,000–₹1,20,000</li>
<li><strong>Laparoscopic hernia repair</strong> with mesh: roughly ₹45,000–₹1,30,000, depending heavily on hernia type and mesh quality</li>
<li><strong>Laparoscopic ovarian cystectomy</strong> (cyst removal, preserving the ovary): roughly ₹40,000–₹1,00,000</li>
<li><strong>Laparoscopic hysterectomy</strong> (uterus removal): roughly ₹60,000–₹1,80,000</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
<p><strong>"Doctor, why does the same gallbladder operation cost ₹45,000 in one hospital and ₹1.5 lakh in another?"</strong></p>
</blockquote>

<p>A fair question, and the answer is rarely that one hospital is cheating you. The differences usually come from room category (general ward versus private room can alone change the bill dramatically), the seniority of the surgical and anaesthesia team, the brand and quality of consumables — a hernia mesh, for example, ranges from basic to premium lightweight varieties — and the city itself, because rents and staffing costs flow into every bill. What matters is that the hospital can show you where your money is going.</p>

<h2>What genuinely moves your bill up or down</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>Complexity of your case.</strong> An acutely inflamed gallbladder or a large, stuck hernia takes longer in theatre than a straightforward one, and theatre time costs money.</li>
<li><strong>Emergency versus planned surgery.</strong> A burst appendix at 2 a.m. involves more than the same appendix removed on a scheduled morning list.</li>
<li><strong>Other health conditions.</strong> Diabetes, heart disease, or obesity may need extra tests, monitoring, or a day more of stay.</li>
<li><strong>Consumables you choose.</strong> Where options exist — mesh grades, energy devices — your surgeon should explain what the costlier option buys you, and when the basic one is perfectly good.</li>
<li><strong>Length of stay.</strong> One advantage of keyhole surgery is that most patients go home in one to two days, which itself keeps costs down compared with open surgery.</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
<p><strong>"Will my insurance cover laparoscopic surgery?"</strong></p>
</blockquote>

<p>In most cases, yes — laparoscopic versions of these operations are standard, medically accepted procedures and are covered by the large majority of health policies, including many government schemes. Do check three things before admission: whether your hospital is on your insurer's cashless network, whether your policy has a waiting period for that condition, and whether there are caps on room rent, because a higher room category can shrink what the insurer pays for everything else. A good hospital billing desk will help you get pre-authorisation sorted before surgery, not after.</p>

<h2>Questions worth asking before you say yes</h2>

<p>When you're handed an estimate, run through this short list. Any hospital doing honest work will answer all five without flinching:</p>

<ul>
<li>Is this a fixed package, or an estimate that can grow? What exactly does it include?</li>
<li>What is <em>not</em> included — implants, pre-operative tests, medicines after discharge?</li>
<li>If a complication extends my stay, how is that charged?</li>
<li>Which mesh or consumable is being used, and were there options?</li>
<li>Who is actually performing the surgery?</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Myth:</strong> "Keyhole surgery is a luxury — open surgery is the economical choice." <strong>Fact:</strong> The operation theatre charges for laparoscopy can be somewhat higher, but the shorter hospital stay, fewer wound complications, and much earlier return to work usually make the <em>total</em> cost of the episode comparable — and often lower once lost wages are counted. Cheap surgery that keeps you in bed for a month isn't cheap.</p>

<h2>Common questions I get asked</h2>

<h3>Is laparoscopic surgery more expensive than open surgery?</h3>
<p>The upfront package is often a little higher because of the equipment involved, but shorter stay and faster recovery narrow or erase that gap. For most people the overall difference is smaller than they expect.</p>

<h3>Why do estimates from two hospitals differ so much for the same operation?</h3>
<p>Room category, team seniority, consumable quality, and city-level costs account for most of the difference. Ask each hospital for an itemised estimate — comparing line by line is far more useful than comparing two lump sums.</p>

<h3>Does health insurance cover laparoscopy in India?</h3>
<p>Yes, in the vast majority of policies these are standard covered procedures. Confirm the cashless network, any waiting periods, and room-rent caps with your insurer before admission.</p>

<h3>Can the final bill exceed the package I was quoted?</h3>
<p>It can, if unexpected findings change the operation or a complication extends your stay. Ask upfront how such situations are charged — a transparent answer to that question tells you a lot about the hospital.</p>
