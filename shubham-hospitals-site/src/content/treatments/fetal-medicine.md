Shubham Hospitals is excited to soon launch a **dedicated Fetal Medicine Centre**, focusing on the **early detection**, **monitoring**, and **treatment** of fetal conditions. Our new unit will bring the latest in advanced prenatal care to **expecting parents**, offering precise diagnostics and personalized care to ensure the healthiest outcomes for both mother and baby.

We are committed to providing comprehensive fetal care services that address every aspect of prenatal health, from **early screenings** to **targeted treatments** for fetal conditions.

## Services

### Detailed Anomaly Scans

Anomaly scans are an essential part of prenatal care, typically performed between 18-22 weeks of pregnancy. These detailed scans provide a comprehensive look at the baby's development, checking for physical abnormalities in organs and structures such as the heart, brain, spine, and kidneys. Early detection of fetal anomalies helps guide medical interventions and provides critical information for parents.

### First-Trimester Screening (NT, Double Marker, NIPT Guidance)

The first-trimester screening is crucial for assessing the risk of chromosomal abnormalities, including Down syndrome and Trisomy 18. Our center will offer NT (Nuchal Translucency) scans, double marker tests, and guidance on NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing). These screenings provide valuable early insight into the pregnancy and help identify those at higher risk for genetic conditions, allowing for timely follow-up and intervention.

### Targeted Doppler Studies

Doppler studies are a non-invasive technique used to assess blood flow in the umbilical cord, placenta, and fetal vessels. This is particularly useful for monitoring pregnancies at high risk or those with concerns about fetal growth. Targeted Doppler studies can detect potential problems, such as insufficient blood supply or fetal growth restriction, allowing for early intervention to ensure the baby's well-being.

### Invasive Diagnostic Procedures (CVS, Amniocentesis)

In certain cases, invasive diagnostic procedures such as Chorionic Villus Sampling (CVS) and amniocentesis may be recommended. These procedures provide genetic testing and are used to detect chromosomal abnormalities or genetic disorders in the fetus. Our experienced team ensures these procedures are performed safely, with the utmost care to minimize risk to both mother and baby.

### Fetal Echocardiography

Fetal echocardiography is a specialized ultrasound technique used to evaluate the baby's heart and detect potential congenital heart defects. It is typically performed if there is a family history of heart conditions, abnormal routine scans, or any signs of fetal heart issues. Early detection allows for timely interventions, which can significantly improve outcomes for babies with congenital heart conditions.

### Fetal Therapy & Counseling

Our Fetal Medicine Centre will also provide fetal therapy and counseling to support parents facing complex prenatal diagnoses. This service will include expert guidance on managing fetal conditions and navigating treatment options, including in-utero therapies and early delivery plans if necessary. We aim to provide compassionate care and support throughout your journey, ensuring informed decision-making for the best possible outcome.
